Chapter 01 · Receipts

We don’t hire resumes. We hire receipts.

The people who do well here have shipped something. They have owned a number. They have stood next to a problem and refused to leave the room until it was fixed.

Where you went to school does not feature in the conversation. Neither does the title you are leaving behind. Show us a repository. A revenue line. A user count. A bad quarter you walked through. A team that would work with you again. If you have receipts, the rest of the interview takes care of itself.

Most hires come through people already on the team. That is by design. The fastest way in is to be referred by an operator who has held the standard with you before. If that is not you yet, write to us anyway. We read every note.

Chapter 02 · The interview

When we meet a candidate, we ask ourselves five questions.

The yes or no on each is the interview. The rest is paperwork.

  1. 01

    Are they smarter than us in the room?

    We hire to be raised, not to feel taller.

  2. 02

    Are they more ambitious than us about the work?

    Personal ambition is normal. Ambition for the work is rare. We need the second.

  3. 03

    Do they take full ownership of their lives?

    The best operators don’t outsource agency. Nothing is anyone else’s fault.

  4. 04

    Are they comfortable saying, “I don’t know”?

    Bluffing is the most expensive habit in any room. We hire people who can stop.

  5. 05

    Will they make every person in the room sharper?

    Talent is a portfolio. The right hire raises the average. The wrong one drops it.

Chapter 03 · Compounding

A decade in, the math changes.

When you give a decade to one operating company, you start watching the consequences of your own decisions land. A feature you shipped in 2018 is still earning the company money in 2026. A junior you hired in 2020 is now the person other juniors come to. The titles stop being interesting. The receipts start stacking.

People who join Inspirelabs in their twenties tend to still be here in their forties. They buy houses on these salaries. They put children through school. They watch the company they helped build cross another zero.

The compounding is not a metaphor. It is the reason to be here in the first place.
Founder’s note.
Chapter 04 · The programmes

How we care for the team.

From day one, the programmes we fund for every full-time teammate. Not perks. Programmes. Funded, run, and reviewed annually.

  1. 01

    24 paid leaves a year.

    Six sick. Six casual. Twelve annual. Plus ten public holidays across the calendar.

  2. 02

    Comprehensive group health cover.

    No cooling period, family-coverage options, annual check-ups for every teammate.

  3. 03

    Health First — the 90-day challenge.

    An annual campaign to build healthy habits. Wellness rooms, recreation zones, and a cafeteria stocked through the day.

  4. 04

    Learn at Work + the Young Leaders Programme.

    Brown-bag sessions, cross-team learning, and a leadership track that hands operators real ownership of outcomes.
