Trust & security at Inspirelabs.

Our standing instruction

Inspirelabs operates a network with 200M+ monthly users, 4,500+ brand partners and a quarter of a billion daily events. The trust those numbers represent is not an output of the business. It is the business.

We treat data security, privacy and platform integrity as a single first-class operating discipline, owned by the CTO, audited every quarter, and held to the same standard the operating company is held to. The pages that follow are the public version of our security posture. The full posture is available under NDA.

Data handling

Five standing instructions every team that touches user data is held to. The list below is the public-facing version. Engineering teams work to a longer internal data-handling spec.

  • Encryption everywhere. AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit. Keys rotated on a published schedule.
  • Least-privilege access. Role-based access controls, mandatory SSO with MFA, and per-environment access reviews every quarter.
  • Tokenised PII. Personal data is tokenised before it crosses a service boundary. Raw PII never leaves the primary store.
  • No cross-product PII leakage. Each property has its own data plane. We do not aggregate user PII across the portfolio.
  • Auditable on demand. Every access, every write, every model decision is logged and audit-trail accessible for at least seven years.

Vulnerability disclosure

Found something? Tell us.

If you have identified a vulnerability in any Inspirelabs property, please write to security@inspirelabs.com with a clear reproduction. We acknowledge inside one business day, triage inside three, and patch on a published severity-based timeline.

We commit to not pursuing legal action against good-faith researchers who follow responsible disclosure. We list named contributors on our public security acknowledgements page once the issue is fixed.
