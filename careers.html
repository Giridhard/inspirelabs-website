We hire operators.
Then we hand them the room.

Twelve years bootstrapped. Five platforms in market. People who joined in their twenties, still here in their forties. The room is built to make you sharper, then it expects you to keep it sharp.

We hire people
who build things
that matter.

The people who do well here have shipped something. They have owned a number. They have stood next to a problem and refused to leave the room until it was fixed.

Where you went to school does not feature in the conversation. Neither does the title you are leaving behind. Show us a repository. A revenue line. A user count. A bad quarter you walked through. A team that would work with you again. If you have receipts, the rest of the interview takes care of itself.

Most hires come through people already on the team. That is by design. The fastest way in is to be referred by an operator who has held the standard with you before. If that is not you yet, write to us anyway. We read every note.

When we meet a candidate, we ask ourselves five questions:

  • Are they smarter than us in the room?
  • Are they more ambitious than us about the work?
  • Do they take full ownership of their lives?
  • Are they comfortable saying, “I don’t know”?
  • Will they make every person in the room sharper?

If that sounds like you, we’d love to meet you.

A decade in, the math changes.

When you give a decade to one operating company, you start watching the consequences of your own decisions land. A feature you shipped in 2018 is still earning the company money in 2026. A junior you hired in 2020 is now the person other juniors come to. The titles stop being interesting. The receipts start stacking.

People who join Inspirelabs in their twenties tend to still be here in their forties. They buy houses on these salaries. They put children through school. They watch the company they helped build cross another zero. The compounding is not a metaphor. It is the reason to be here in the first place.
