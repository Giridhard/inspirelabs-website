Careers

We hire operators. Then we hand them the room.

Twelve years bootstrapped, five platforms in market, people who joined in their twenties still here in their forties. The room is built to make you sharper, then it expects you to keep it sharp.

We do not hire resumes. We hire receipts.

The people who do well here have shipped something. They have owned a number. They have stood next to a problem and refused to leave the room until it was fixed. Where you went to school does not feature in the conversation. Neither does the title you are leaving behind.

What we look for is simple. Evidence. A repository. A revenue line. A user count. A story about a bad quarter you walked through. A team that would work with you again. If you have receipts, the rest of the interview takes care of itself.

Most hires come through people already on the team. That is by design. The fastest way in is to be referred by an operator who has held the standard with you before. If that is not you yet, write to us anyway. We read every note.

When we meet a candidate, we ask ourselves five questions.

The yes or no on each is the interview. The rest is paperwork.

01

Are they smarter than us in the room?

02

Are they more ambitious than us about the work?

03

Do they take full ownership of their lives?

04

Are they comfortable saying, “I don’t know”?

05

Will they make every person in the room sharper?

A decade in, the math changes.

When you give a decade to one operating company, you start watching the consequences of your own decisions land. A feature you shipped in 2018 is still earning the company money in 2026. A junior you hired in 2020 is now the person other juniors come to. The titles stop being interesting. The receipts start stacking.

People who join Inspirelabs in their twenties tend to still be here in their forties. They buy houses on these salaries. They put children through school. They watch the company they helped build cross another zero. The compounding is not a metaphor. It is the reason to be here in the first place.

How we care for the team.

From day one, the programmes we fund for every full-time teammate.

Leave
24 paid leaves a year. Six sick. Six casual. Twelve annual. On top of ten public holidays across the calendar.
Health
Comprehensive health insurance. Group coverage with no cooling period, family-coverage options, and annual check-ups for every teammate.
Wellness
Health First, the 90-day challenge. An annual campaign to build healthy habits, supported by wellness rooms, recreation zones, and a cafeteria stocked through the day.
Growth
Learn at Work and the Young Leaders Programme. Brown-bag sessions, cross-team learning, and a leadership track that hands operators real ownership of outcomes.
Belonging
Festivals from every tradition. Iftars, Diwali, Christmas, Women’s Day, Sports Week, annual outings. Part of the year, not a calendar afterthought.
The room

This place is built to make you sharper.

The work asks for hard hours, real ownership, and the willingness to sit slightly outside your comfort. You will feel a little stretched. A little under-prepared for the size of the problem in front of you. That is the room. That is also the only room where the compounding happens.
